APPOINTMENTS STRENGTHEN ADMINISTRATIVE APPEALS TRIBUNAL

Attorney-General Philip Ruddock today announced the appointment of four new  part-time members and the re-appointment of four current members to the  Administrative Appeals Tribunal.

“It gives me great pleasure to welcome Dr Kerry Breen, Dr Timothy Hawcroft,  Mr Timothy Jenkins and Professor Peter Reilly AO to the Tribunal as part-time  members,” Mr Ruddock said.

“These new appointees are well respected members of their chosen professions, in  fields as diverse as medicine, actuarial practice and veterinary science.

“I am delighted persons of such calibre are willing to make further valuable  contributions to the community by bringing their experience and expertise to the  Tribunal,” he said.

Dr Kerry Breen will join the Tribunal in Victoria, Dr Hawcroft and Mr Jenkins will be  based in New South Wales and Professor Reilly will be located in South Australia.

“I also acknowledge the re-appointment of Mr Deane Jarvis as a full-time Deputy  President in South Australia, Mr Bruce Pascoe as a part-time senior member in  Victoria, Dr John Campbell as a part-time member in New South Wales and Ms Ann  Cunningham, who will be moving from the role of part-time member to part-time  senior member in Tasmania,” Mr Ruddock said.

“These members represent a wealth of experience and knowledge and have  demonstrated a deep commitment to the importance of administrative review and to  the performance and good reputation of the Tribunal.

“I am pleased the Tribunal and the community will continue to benefit from their  dedication and contributions to the development of best practice,” he said.

All the appointments and re-appointments will commence on 1 July 2006.

The Administrative Appeals Tribunal provides independent review of a wide range  of administrative decisions made by the Australian Government.

* Biographical information on the appointees and re-appointees follows.

Mr Deane Jarvis

Full-time Deputy President, South Australia (1 July 2006 - 31 May 2011)

Mr Jarvis has been Deputy President and Co-ordinator for the Administrative Appeals  Tribunal in South Australia since July 2003. He is currently vice-convenor of the South  Australian chapter of the Council of Australasian Tribunals.  From 1991 until 2003 he was  senior partner with Kelly & Co, Adelaide. He was a partner with Corrs Chambers Westgarth  from 1989 until 1991 and partner with Mollison Litchfield from 1969 until 1989.  Mr Jarvis  was also chair of Bridgestone Australia Ltd from 1992 to 1998.  He was a council member of  the Law Society of South Australia from 1973 until 1983 and chair of a number of its  committees. Mr Jarvis has a Bachelor of Laws from the University of Adelaide and is a fellow  of the Australian Institute of Company Directors. He was admitted as a barrister and  solicitor of the Supreme Court of South Australia and has been on the roll of the High Court  of Australia since 1964.



Ms Ann Cunningham

Part-time senior member, Tasmania (1 July 2006 - 31 May 2011)

Ms Cunningham has been a part-time member of the Tribunal in Tasmania since 1995.  She  was appointed chair of the Public Trustee Board (Tasmania) in 2004. She has been a part-time  presiding member of the Tasmanian Resource Management and Planning Appeals Tribunal  since 1996, and deputy president of the Tasmanian Mental Health Tribunal since 1999. She  acts as a sessional mediator with the Supreme Court of Tasmania, as a complaints  commissioner for the University of Tasmania and has acted as a conciliator for the Legal Aid  Commission of Tasmania. She practised as a legal practitioner for nine years, from 1980-1983  and from 2000-2004. She has been a deputy registrar (part-time) of the Family Court of  Australia (1984-1999), coordinator of the Family Mediation Service (1995) and a sessional  mediator for Relationships Australia (1991-1995).  She has a Bachelor of Laws (Hons) from  the University of Tasmania.  She is also an approved arbitrator and mediator.  She has been  enrolled as a legal practitioner of the Supreme Court of Tasmania since 1980.



Mr Bruce Pascoe

Part-time senior member, Victoria (1 July 2006 - 31 May 2009)

Mr Pascoe was appointed as a part-time member of the Tribunal in 1991 and has been a  part-time senior member since 1995. He is also currently director of A.F Gason Pty Ltd.  Prior  to joining the Tribunal, he was a partner at Ernst & Young (1965-1991) and before that, a  generalist chartered accountant. He began specialising in taxation in 1960. He was chair of  the Tax Agents Board (Victoria) from 1972 until 1997, president of the Taxation Institute of  Australia from 1976 until 1978, treasurer of the Spastic Society of Victoria from 1991 until  1996 and treasurer of the Institute of Arbitrators & Mediators Australia from 2002 until 2004.  He is a fellow of the Institute of Chartered Accountants Australia and fellow and life  member of the Taxation Institute of Australia.  He is an arbitrator (Grade 1) and accredited  mediator with the Institute of Arbitrators & Mediators Australia.

Dr John Campbell

Part-time member, New South Wales (1 July 2006 - 31 May 2011)

Dr Campbell has been a part-time member of the Tribunal since 1991.  He is the chair of, and  a senior consultant to, MA International Pty Ltd, a company consulting in health  management issues. During 1996-97, he was managing director and board member of  AusHealth International Pty Ltd. In 1995 he was appointed to the position of Chief Executive  Officer and board member of South Eastern Sydney Areas Health Service, having held the  position of CEO and board member of the Southern Sydney Area Health Service from 1988  until 1995. He was a member of the Royal Australian Army Medical Corps from 1962 until  1980. He is a former board member of the NRMA (1997-2001), member of the Ministerial  Advisory Committee for Health (1993-95) and director of the Australian Council on Health  Care Standards (1988-94). He has a Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery from  Adelaide University, a Diploma of Tropical Medicine and Health from Sydney University, a  Master of Health Administration from the University of New South Wales (UNSW), a  Bachelor of Laws from UNSW and Master of Laws from Sydney University. He is a fellow of  the Royal Australian College of Medical Administrators, Australian Institute of Management  and Australian Institute of Company Directors.



Dr Kerry Breen

Part-time member, Victoria (1 July 2006 - 31 May 2011)

Dr Breen is a former Director of the Department of Gastroenterology at St Vincent’s Hospital  Melbourne and has been in private medical practice since 1996. He is an associate professor  in the Department of Medicine, University of Melbourne and honorary associate in the  Department of Forensic Medicine, Monash University. He has been chair of the Australian  Health Ethics Committee and member of the National Health and Medical Research Council  from 2000-2006. He is a former member (1981-90), deputy president (1991-94) and president  (1994-2000) of the Medical Practitioners Board of Victoria and a former president of the  Australian Medical Council (1997-2000). He has co-authored a number of books on law and  ethics in medicine. He has a Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery and Doctorate in  Medicine from the University of Melbourne.



Dr Timothy Hawcroft

Part-time member, New South Wales (1 July 2006 - 31 May 2011)

Dr Hawcroft is a qualified veterinarian who established and was principal of the Gordon  Veterinarian Hospital from 1971 to 2002. He was also a part-time consultant for the  University Veterinary Centre, Sydney, during 2003, and Honorary Veterinarian for the Royal  Agricultural Society of NSW from 1975 until 1986. He commenced his career as a veterinary  surgeon at the Gladesville Veterinary Hospital where he worked from 1969 until 1971. He is  an accredited veterinarian with the Australian Quarantine Inspection Service. He is the  author of a number of popular books on veterinary science and animal care, and has  presented papers at conferences and meetings. He graduated with a Bachelor of Veterinary  Science (Hons) from the University of Sydney in 1969. He is a life member of the Australian  Veterinary Association and the Australian College of Veterinary Scientists.

Mr Timothy Jenkins

Part-time member, New South Wales (1 July 2006 - 31 May 2011)

Mr Jenkins has been a partner (emeritus) and consulting actuary with  PricewaterhouseCoopers since 2003, having been a partner from 1999 until 2002. He was  chief executive of Superannuation and Insurance at Westpac from 1996 until 1998, having  been general manager of Westpac Financial Services from 1993 until 1996. He was managing  director of ANZ Funds Management from 1989 until 1993 and managing director of Greater  Pacific Life, before it became ANZ Life, from 1979 until 1993. Prior to this, he was a partner  with E S Knight & Co, Consulting Actuaries (1970-76) and before that was employed by  MLC Life (1960-70). He has been chair of the Life Insurance Actuarial Standards Board since  1998. He is a former president of the Institute of Actuaries of Australia and current convenor  of the Institute’s Professional Conduct Committee. He was elected as a life member of the  Institute of Actuaries of Australia in 2005. He qualified as an actuary by satisfying  requirements for admission as a fellow of the Institute of Actuaries (London) in 1965.



Professor Peter Reilly AO

Part-time member, South Australia (1 July 2006 - 31 May 2011)

Professor Reilly has been a practising neurosurgeon since 1976 and is currently a visiting  neurosurgeon at the Royal Adelaide Hospital. From 1994 until 2005 he was head of the  Department of Neurosurgery having been previously Director of Neurosurgical Research  (1985-94) and senior staff specialist (1980-85) at the Royal Adelaide Hospital. He has also  been a visiting staff specialist at Flinders Medical Centre, Adelaide Women’s and Children’s  Hospital and Royal Darwin Hospital. Since 2004 he has been clinical professor of  Neurosurgery in the Department of Surgery at the University of Adelaide. He is an executive  member and secretary of the Neurosurgical Research Foundation Inc. He has recently  completed a term as president of the International Neurotrauma Society. He is also a former  president (1996-98) of the Neurosurgical Society of Australasia, chair (1991-94) of the  Neurosurgical Board of the Royal Australasian College of Surgeons and supervisor of  Neurosurgical Training for South Australia (1994-2000 and at present). He has a Bachelor of  Medical Science, Doctorate of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery from the University of  Adelaide. He is a fellow of the Royal Australasian College of Surgeons and of the Faculty of  Pain Medicine. He was made an Officer in the General Division of the Order of Australia in  2002 for service to the advancement of neurosurgery through clinical practice, research,  education, and professional organisations, and in the prevention and treatment of head  injuries.
