SHADOW MINISTER FO R IMMIGRATION

TO VISIT CAMBODIA

Shadow Minister for Immigration and Ethnic Affairs Mr Philip Ruddock MP  is to visit Cambodia next week. H e will leave on Sunday, May 17 and return  on Friday, May 22. *

Mr Ruddock will be accompanied by Senator Robert Hill, Shadow Minister  for Foreign Affairs and Mr Bob Halverson, Opposition Whip.

Mrs Cathy Sullivan will be acting Shadow Minister in M r Ruddock's

This will be Mr Ruddock's third Cambodian visit in the past five years. He  will be meeting with H E Hun Sen, H R H  Prince Sihanouk and H E  Son Sann  to discuss the advancement of the peace process and witness first hand the  recent political developments.

Mr Ruddock will also be visiting Kompon Speu to assess the United Nations  refugee resettlement program. He will be meeting with representatives from  a range of Non Government Organisations and other United Nations  agencies which are assisting in the programs as well as representatives from

the Thai Government.

"These visits to the refugee resettlement areas and meetings with  participating agencies, will provide me with a greater understanding of the  general situation in Cambodia and allow me to make a better assessment of  the claims of Khmer asylum seekers in Australia," Mr Ruddock said.

"It is vitally important that we know the situation in Cambodia so that we  can make the appropriate decisions."
