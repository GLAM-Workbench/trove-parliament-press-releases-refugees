

MEDIA RELEASE MINISTER FOR FOREIGN AFFAIRS ALEXANDER DOWNER FA45                2 May 2003





DIPLOMATIC APPOINTMENT - IRAQ

I have today announced that Mr Neil Mules, a senior career officer with the Department of  Foreign Affairs and Trade Australia, has been appointed to head the Australian  Representative Office in Baghdad.

A joint DFAT/Defence team has been in Baghdad this week making an assessment of the  security and infrastructure situation.  The process of opening the office will begin from  around the end of next week.  It will be the first resident Australian mission in Baghdad since  1991.

I expect to visit Baghdad and open the mission later this month.The office will strengthen our  ability to coordinate our rehabilitation efforts with our coalition partners and the Iraqi people.   Australia wants to play a positive and constructive role in efforts to help the Iraqi people build  a brighter and more prosperous future.

Mr Mules joined the Department in 1980.  He has served as Counsellor, Hanoi (1996-98),  Counsellor, Jakarta (1992-95), First Secretary, Harare (1988-90), First Secretary, Ankara  (1985-88), and Third Secretary/later Second Secretary, Brasilia (1981-84).

In Canberra he was First Assistant Secretary, Diplomatic Security, Property and Information  Management Division (2000-02), Assistant Secretary, Maritime South East Asia Branch  (1998-2001), Director, Conditions of Service Section (1991-92) and Desk Officer, Refugees,  Immigration and Asylum Section (1984-85).

Mr Mules has a Bachelor of Arts degree from Flinders University, Adelaide, South Australia.
